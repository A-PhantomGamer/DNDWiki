Campaign Lore

Legends & Myths

The Forge of Spells

Ancient Legend

Centuries ago, the dwarves and gnomes of Phandelver's Pact built a magical forge where enchanted items could be crafted. The most talented smiths from both races gathered to share their secrets and create wonders that would never again be replicated after the forge was lost.

It is said that items crafted in the Forge of Spells were imbued with extraordinary powers, and that the forge itself was powered by magical energy drawn from the far reaches of the multiverse.

Source: Dwarven Chronicles

The Curse of Strahd

Dark Legend

The tale of Strahd von Zarovich is one of tragic love and terrible darkness. Once a noble warrior, his obsession with a young woman named Tatyana led him to make a pact with dark powers. On the day of his brother's wedding to Tatyana, Strahd murdered his brother and pursued Tatyana until she flung herself from the walls of Castle Ravenloft.

In that moment, Strahd became a vampire, and the land of Barovia was torn from the material plane, surrounded by deadly mists that trap all who enter. It is said that Strahd is doomed to find Tatyana's reincarnation again and again, only to lose her each time.

Source: Vistani Tales

The Ordning

Giant Mythology

The giants of Faerûn follow a strict hierarchical structure known as the Ordning, established by their god Annam the All-Father. Storm giants stand at the top, followed by cloud, fire, frost, stone, and hill giants.

When the Ordning was shattered, giants of all types began acting independently, seeking ways to elevate themselves in the eyes of Annam. Some sought ancient relics, others built massive structures, and the most aggressive began raiding settlements of the small folk.

Source: Giant Sagas

Historical Events

1372 DR

The Spellplague

When the goddess Mystra was murdered, magic itself went wild. Blue flames swept across Faerûn, transforming landscapes, creatures, and even people with unpredictable magical effects. Entire nations were lost, and new ones were created in their place.

Major World Event
1385 DR

The Sundering

A century after the Spellplague, the worlds of Abeir and Toril, long separated, began to overlap again. This cosmic event, known as the Sundering, was prophesied by Ao the Overgod. It resulted in the return of many deities thought lost and the restoration of the Weave of magic to its natural state.

Cosmic Event
1491 DR

The Breaking of the Ordning

For reasons unknown to even the giants themselves, Annam the All-Father shattered the Ordning, the divine hierarchy that had governed giant society for millennia. This caused chaos among giantkind as they sought to establish a new order and prove their worth to their god.

Regional Event

Artifacts & Magic

The Sunsword

Legendary Weapon

A legendary sun blade once wielded by Sergei von Zarovich, Strahd's brother. The weapon was broken long ago, but its hilt still contains the power to conjure a blade of pure radiance that is especially effective against undead.

Properties
  • Conjures a blade of pure radiance
  • Deals additional radiant damage to undead
  • Emits bright sunlight in a 15-foot radius
  • Sentient weapon with its own personality
History

The Sunsword was crafted for Sergei von Zarovich as a wedding gift. After Strahd murdered his brother, the sword was hidden away. It was later broken, and its hilt separated from the blade. The party recovered the hilt from the wizard Khazan's tower and restored its power.

Current Location: In party's possession

Dragonstaff of Ahghairon

Unique Staff

A wooden staff topped with a carving of a dragon's head. Created by Ahghairon, the first Lord Magister of Waterdeep, this staff grants its wielder power over dragons.

Properties
  • Dragons must make a Wisdom save or be charmed for 1 minute
  • Grants advantage on Charisma checks against dragons
  • Can cast Dominate Monster on dragons once per day
History

Ahghairon created this staff to help establish peaceful relations with the dragons near Waterdeep. It was lost during a dragon attack on the city centuries ago and was recently recovered from a hoard in the Spine of the World mountains.

Current Location: Unknown

Religions & Deities

Tyr

The Even-Handed, God of Justice

Alignment: Lawful Good

Domains: Justice, Law, War

Symbol: Balanced scales resting on a warhammer

Tyr is the god of justice and law. He appears as a noble warrior missing his right hand, which he sacrificed to bind the wolf Fenrir. His followers value honor, fairness, and duty above all else.

Worship

Temples to Tyr serve as courthouses and halls of justice. His priests often serve as judges, arbiters, and peacekeepers. They are expected to be fair and impartial in all their dealings, punishing the guilty and protecting the innocent regardless of status or wealth.

Major deity in the Sword Coast

Lathander

The Morninglord, God of Dawn

Alignment: Neutral Good

Domains: Life, Light, Renewal

Symbol: Rising sun

Lathander is the deity of creativity, dawn, renewal, birth, athletics, spring, self-perfection, vitality, and youth. He is known as the Morninglord, and he encourages new endeavors, ideas, and hope.

Worship

Temples of Lathander face east to catch the first rays of the rising sun. Services are held at dawn and involve singing and offerings of fresh flowers and wine. His priests are optimistic and energetic, always looking forward to a bright future.

Widely worshipped across Faerûn

DM Secrets

The True Identity of the Black Spider

Plot Twist

The Black Spider is actually Nezznar, a drow who serves a much larger organization known as the Kraken Society. His mission to secure the Forge of Spells is just one part of a larger plan to collect magical artifacts across the Sword Coast.

If the players defeat him but don't kill him, he can become a recurring villain who seeks revenge while reporting back to his mysterious superiors.

Not Yet Revealed

The True Nature of Ireena Kolyana

Character Secret

Ireena is not just a random reincarnation of Tatyana - she is actually carrying a fragment of the divine spark that once belonged to a forgotten goddess of the dawn. This is why Strahd is drawn to her but can never fully possess her.

If the players help her realize her true nature, she could potentially break Strahd's curse and free Barovia from the mists.
